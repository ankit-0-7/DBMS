ZAAM ARIF

PAKISTANI, B. 1999

Zaam Arif, born in 1999 in Karachi, is a self-taught artist influenced by his painter parents. His art reflects philosophical and literary inspirations, evolving into a profound exploration of the human experience. Arif's work, characterized by a synergy between characters and their environment, has been showcased in solo exhibitions in the UK, the US, and Pakistan. Notable upcoming events include his first solo exhibition in India at Vadehra Art Gallery, New Delhi (2024), and a viewing at the Cabin LA, Los Angeles (2024). Arif has also participated in group exhibitions globally. His work gained recognition in The New Yorker magazine in 2021, making him the youngest Pakistani artist featured. In 2021, he received the Malamegi Lab Research Grant in Italy and completed a residency at La Brea in Los Angeles in 2023. Currently based in Houston, Texas, Zaam Arif continues to make a mark in the art world.

SUNIL GUPTA

INDIAN, B. 1953

Sunil Gupta, an Indian–British–Canadian photographer born in 1953, has been a critical practitioner of independent photography for over five decades, focusing on race, migration, and queer issues. His extensive solo exhibitions and retrospectives, showcased globally, include venues such as the Photographers’ Gallery in London and the Ryerson Image Centre in Toronto. Active in group exhibitions at renowned institutions like Frieze, Tate Britain, and the National Gallery of Art, Gupta has authored notable publications, including "Come Out" and "We Were Here: Sexuality, Photography, and Cultural Difference." His work is held in prestigious collections worldwide, including the Tokyo Museum of Photography, Victoria & Albert Museum, Tate Modern, and Harvard University. Currently living and working in London, Gupta is a professorial fellow at UCA, Farnham, and holds teaching roles at Kingston University and the Royal College of Art. He served as lead curator for the Houston Fotofest in 2018.

SHAILESH B.R.

INDIAN, B. 1986

Shailesh B.R., born in 1986 in Karnataka, is a mixed-media artist known for collage and kinetic sculptures. He holds a degree in fine arts and explores spatial dynamics inspired by artists like Mukherjee and Matisse. Exhibiting globally, Shailesh's works touch on AI and enlightenment. Solo shows include "The Last Brahmin" in Nice, France (2020). In 2023, he was a resident at the Institute of Science and Technology Austria. A finalist for the Sovereign Art Prize in 2021, Shailesh lives and works in New Delhi, India.

JASMINE NILANI JOSEPH

SRI LANKAN, B. 1990

Jasmine Nilani Joseph, born in Jaffna in 1990, holds a bachelor’s degree in fine arts from the University of Jaffna (2015). Notable for her first solo exhibition at the University of Jaffna (2019) and an open-studio exhibition at Pepper House, Kochi (2020), she has exhibited globally, including venues like Gropius Bau, Berlin (2023), and Vadehra Art Gallery, New Delhi (2023). Her work is featured in prestigious publications like Phaidon’s "Vitamin D3." Winner of the DBF Asia Art Future Award (2022), Joseph resides and works in Sri Lanka, with her art included in private collections.
